Black Forest Cake

When we talk about Germany and which dessert represents them, Black Forest Cake (or as its known in Germany - “Schwarzwälder Kirschtorte.”) is the one to go with.
With layers of chocolate, at least four of them, light and fluffy whipped cream and sour/sweet cherries, there is not a single person that could resist such goodiness.

The best time to make it is always, but lets keep it real, when the cherries get discounted, there is no better time to
whip some good ol` cream and added chocolate base.


This is not for children! Well, it will not be if you do use the special ingredient, and that is cherry liquor.

After a good amount of time spent in the fridge, it will be ready to enjoy to the fullest!

Chocolate base

  1. Oven and cake pans: Preheat oven to 180°C / 350°F (160°C fan). Grease 3 x 20cm (8”) cake pans with butter, line with parchment / baking paper.
  2. Sift dry ingredients: Combine dry ingredients by sifting the cocoa and plain flour into a bowl. Set aside.
  3. Beat eggs: Beat eggs for 30 seconds on speed 6 of a stand mixer fitted with a whisk attachment, or hand beater.
  4. Slowly add sugar: With the beater still going, slowly pour the sugar in over 45 seconds, then add the vanilla.
  5. Beat 7 minutes: Beat for 7 minutes more on speed 8, or until tripled in volume and pale in colour. Don't shortcut this step; this is what makes the cake rise (there's no baking powder used).
  6. Fold in dry ingredients: Add the flour and cocoa mixture to the batter, folding in with a spatula until just combined (a few streaks of flour remaining is fine). Be gentle here, we don't want to knock out the air bubbles.
  7. Fold in butter: Add the butter and fold gently through until just combined. Once you can no longer see any flour, stop stirring.
  8. Fill cake pans: Pour the batter into the three prepared pans. The batter should be fairly thin and pourable.
  9. Bake: Place into the oven and bake for 25 minutes, until springy to the touch and a skewer inserted into the cake centre comes out clean. (Note 5 regarding shelf placement).
  10. Cool: Remove from the oven. Cool in the pan for 10 minutes before turning the cakes out onto a rack to cool completely.

Cherry syrup

  1. Drain cherries: And reserve liquid.
  2. Cherry cornflour slurry: Measure out 1/4 cup (60ml) of the reserved cherry juice and mix with the cornflour to make a slurry. Set aside.
  3. Make cherry syrup: Place a medium pot over medium-low heat. Add the sugar and another 1/3 cup of reserved juice. Bring to a gentle simmer to dissolve the sugar. Stir in the cornflour slurry and bring to a simmer. Cook for 1 minute, stirring, until it thickens into a thin syrup.
  4. Soak cherries, cool: Remove from the heat and stir in the kirsch. Pour the syrup over the drained cherries and allow to cool completely before using.

Whip cream

  1. Mix it up: Just prior to assembling, place cream, icing sugar and vanilla in a large bowl. Whip into pretty stiff peaks – about 5 minutes on high. Refrigerate until needed.

Construct

  1. Place one cake layer upside down on a serving platter
  2. Brush with cherry syrup: Brush the cake layer with 1/4 cup of the cherry syrup the cherries are soaking in.
  3. Cream layer: Spread with 1 cup of whipped cream, leaving a 1cm (1/2") border (the weight of the next cake layer gently pressed will push cream to edge).
  4. Layer cherries: Top the cream with half the cherries (in a single layer), using a slotted spoon to drain well (but don't pat dry, want the cherries juicy!).
  5. Repeat: Top with another cake layer, brush with 1/4 cup syrup, spread with 1 cup cream, top with remaining cherries, and place 3rd cake layer on top.
  6. Cover with cream: Reserve 1 1/2 cups whipped cream for piping cream decorations. Spread the remaining cream over the top and side of the cake. Pipe dollops around the edge of top using a large star-tipped nozzle. Top each dollop with a maraschino cherry.
  7. Rest 4 hours: Leave cake in the fridge for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight, to allow flavours to develop and syrup to really soak into the cake.
  8. Take out of fridge: Remove from fridge 30 minutes prior to serving (but be mindful of cream melting on hot days).

Recipe Notes

  1. Use canned/jared cherries, but for an alternative you can always use frozen pitted cherries. Thaw them completely to reserve liquid and add cherry juice for the liquid in the recipe to make the syrup
  2. Eggs need to be at room temperature, to ensure it incorporates properly into the batter, because they aerate better when beaten.
  3. Storage and serving - keeps 3-4 days in the fridge, but keep it covered. Take it out 30 mins prior to serving.
